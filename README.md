HR Analytics Dashboard | Attrition Analysis | EXCEL-SQL-TABLEAU-POWER BI

~ Project Overview
This project focuses on HR attrition analysis to understand employee turnover patterns, identify high-risk groups, and uncover key factors influencing attrition such as age, department, gender, education, and job satisfaction. The dashboards helps HR teams make data-driven retention decisions.

~ Attrition Highlights
1. Younger employees tend to switch jobs more frequently
2. Attrition reduces as age and experience increase
3. Job roles such as Sales Executive & Research Scientist show higher exits
4. Strong link between job satisfaction and retention


~Business Objectives
1. Measure overall attrition rate
2. Identify departments with highest attrition
3. Analyze attrition by age group and gender
4. Understand impact of education field & job satisfaction
5. Track active vs exited employees
6. Enable HR leaders to take preventive retention actions

~ Tools I Used

1. SQL (PostgreSQL) – Data cleaning & analysis
2. Power BI – Interactive HR dashboard
3. Tableau – Alternate visualization version
4. Excel / CSV – Source dataset
5. GitHub – Documentation & project versioning

~ Key HR Metrics
1. Total Employees: 1,470
2. Employees Left (Attrition): 237
3. Attrition Rate: 16.12%
4. Active Employees: 1,233
5. Average Employee Age: 37 years

~ Key Insights
1. R&D department has the highest attrition (~56%)
2. Male employees show higher attrition compared to females
3. Age group 25–34 records the highest attrition count
4. Employees from Life Sciences & Medical backgrounds leave more frequently
5. Low job satisfaction (ratings 1–2) correlates strongly with attrition
6. Attrition significantly decreases for employees aged 45+
